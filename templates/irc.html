Introduction to Lojbanic IRC

There are usually a couple dozen people in #lojban at any given time, but they often aren't engaged in active discussion. Say coi, and wait. Get a soda. Practice your vocabulary. But be patient. Dedicated IRCers don't sit in front of the computer 24/7 waiting to respond within 10 seconds of everything said. Think of it as sort of a fast moving message board.

If you have used IRC before, then you'll probably have an IRC client already installed. Connect to irc.lojban.org, port 6667 and join the channel #lojban.

If, on the other hand, you are new to IRC, you can access the channel through your web browser. You'll probably find this IRC tutorial useful as well.

Cheat Sheet

Even though a great deal of conversation happens in English in #lojban, you'll still see plenty of Lojban. Here's a quick summary of the most common phrases you are likely to see.

Lojban English
coi hi.
coi ro do hi everyone.
co'o bye.
co'o rodo bye everyone.
do mo what's up?
go'i yes (repeat what you just said)
na go'i no
je'e roger that
fi'i make yourself at home
mi jimpe I understand
mi na jimpe I don't understand
mi'e .maik. My name is Michael
ki'a what?!
ki'e thanks
.u'u sorry
.u'u sai very sorry
.u'u cai do cevni fi mi very very sorry
.e'u "I suggest.." or "please..."
xamgu good
po'u which is...
ko gletu lo xasli I am now a true Lojbanist (rite of passage)
.ie agree
.ie nai disagree
.ui :-) (happy)
.ui nai :-(
.u'i :-D lol
.u'i nai :-(!! Not funny.
zo'o :-) (only joking)
zo'o nai :-[ (serious!)
.ua oh! (discovery)
.ua nai I'm confused
mi na jundi brb / afk
le mi zdani cu fagri I love IRC so much that nothing else matters.
mi co'u jundi back again
mi prami do I love you
